## closing-trajectory-soler
type: research-trajectory

### eyebrow
Where this argument extends

### author
Sara Soler i Arjona

### work_title
"'To Live Is a Matter of Time': Memory, Survival and Queer Refugeehood in Ocean Vuong's *On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous*"

### quote
By queering the normative temporality of the refugee experience, the novel envisions another way of "living historically," showcasing how the characters' refugeehood is not finite but ongoing, necessitating a continuous search for healing and resilience.

### citation
Soler i Arjona 3

### role
Soler i Arjona reads refugeehood in the novel as a queered, ongoing temporality rather than a finite arrival. That frame extends this talk's exposure work into CI 2's love-and-harm bind. If refugee time keeps reopening, then being seen on America's terms is never closed either, and the demand to be legible keeps returning across generations.

## closing-trajectory-lee
type: research-trajectory

### eyebrow
Where this argument extends

### author
Rachel Lee

### work_title
"Affective Chemistries of Care: Slow Activism and the Limits of the Molecular in Ocean Vuong's *On Earth We Are Briefly Gorgeous*"

### quote
On Earth portrays the lingering afterlives of the military-poison complex and the narcotic-pharmaceutical industries as both of these intersect with structures of settler-colonialism, late capitalism, and a biopolitics invested in the life extension of white men.

### citation
Lee 194

### role
Lee reframes the violence in the novel as chemical and biopolitical, the slow afterlife of Vietnam-era defoliants and the U.S. opioid economy. That reading pushes CI 1's hunter-prey scenes past metaphor. The macaque hunt and the soldier's reading are events inside a longer regime that targets some bodies for harm and others for preservation.

## closing-thesis
type: thesis-reveal

### thesis
Through the hunter-prey motif, the novel exposes the majoritarian demand that marginalized people become readable on America's terms and analyzes being seen as the source of both love and harm. It challenges that demand through the letter itself, where parataxis and reclaimed language let Little Dog build a new sense of self on his own terms.

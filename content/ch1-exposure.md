## ch1-macaque
type: quote-block

### quote
The shots are held by arms that belong to men who will soon cut open the macaque's skull with a scalpel, open it like a lid on a jar. The men will take turns consuming the brain, dipped in alcohol or swallowed with cloves of garlic from a porcelain plate, all while the monkey kicks beneath them.

### citation
Vuong 43

### setup
Early in the novel Vuong braids two scenes into one prose flow with no chapter break.

### analysis
Five men in a fluorescent-lit garage are about to eat the brain of a live macaque. The civility is the cover the killing wears.

## ch1-lens
type: lens-bundle

### methodology_quote
privileges Whites, men, the middle and/or upper class, and heterosexuals by naming these social locations as natural or normative points of reference

### methodology_citation
Solórzano and Yosso 28

### lens_quote
To maintain white racial comfort, white spectators ignore the adverse impacts of well-intentioned actions.

### lens_citation
Snyder-Young 89

### analysis
Solórzano and Yosso name the dominant point of reference: white, male, middle-class, straight. Snyder-Young names the cover that lets the verdict feel like mercy — a well-intentioned action keeps its own adverse impact invisible. The cinnamon tint and the blond temples are doing the work of recognition. Rose only lives because the soldier reads the same logic onto her that kills the macaque.
